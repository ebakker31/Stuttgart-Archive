# Pricing Strategy — FitLedger

## Recommended price: **$39 one-time** (launch intro $29)

### Anchors (cited in `/01_research/source_notes.md`)

| Reference point | Price |
|---|---|
| MyFitnessPal Premium | $79.99/yr ($19.99/mo) |
| MyFitnessPal Premium+ | $99.99/yr |
| MacroFactor | $71.99/yr ($11.99/mo), no free tier |
| HealthFit (one-time iOS data app) | $2.99 — proves one-time purchases sell in this space |
| Etsy tracker spreadsheets | $5–25 (quality floor to stay above) |

### Logic

- **$39** = ~half of one year of either market leader; high enough to signal "real software,"
  low enough for an impulse-adjacent 18–35 purchase.
- Below $29 it reads like a spreadsheet; above $59 it invites feature-for-feature comparison
  against subscription apps with bigger databases — a fight v1 shouldn't pick.
- One-time pricing **is the product positioning**, not just a number — the entire wedge is
  anti-subscription.

### Margin / costs

Static product, no per-user infra. Platform fees only: Gumroad ~10%; Shopify $39/mo + payment
fees; Lemon Squeezy ~5% + 50¢ (handles EU VAT as merchant of record — material for digital
goods). Net margin ≥85% on every channel.

### Price tests (Phase 8 detail)

1. Launch at $29 intro → settle at $39 list. 2. Test $39 vs $44 after 100 sales (whole-dollar,
   non-gimmick). 3. Pro tier $59–79 only once Strava sync ships. Never discount below $24 —
   protects refund goodwill and perceived quality.
