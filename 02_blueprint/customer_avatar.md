# Customer Avatar — FitLedger

## Primary avatar: "The Spreadsheet Lifter" (60% of buyers)

- **Age/profile:** 22–32, lifts 3–5×/week, runs occasionally; tech-comfortable; active on
  fitness TikTok/Reddit (r/fitness, r/weightroom, r/naturalbodybuilding).
- **Current stack:** a Google Sheets workout log they half-maintain, a macro app free tier they
  resent (ads, paywalled barcode scanning), maybe Strava free.
- **Pain:** paying $72–100/yr per app feels like a scam for "a database and a chart"; data is
  scattered across three apps that don't talk; canceling a subscription means losing history.
- **Trigger moments:** annual renewal email; app paywalls a feature they used; New Year /
  pre-summer cut; seeing "you don't need a $20/month app" content on TikTok.
- **Objections:** "Why pay when free apps exist?" → ownership, no ads, all three domains in one.
  "Will I lose my data if I clear my browser?" → backup/restore is first-class. "Is the food
  database big enough?" → honest answer: curated + custom foods, not 19M crowdsourced entries.

## Secondary avatar: "The Hybrid Self-Coacher" (30%)

- 25–35, trains for HYROX-style events or runs + lifts; owns a Garmin/Apple Watch; exports and
  analyzes their own data; privacy-conscious; hates that their training history is hostage to
  five platforms.
- Buys for: universal import (GPX/TCX/CSV), one dashboard across lifting + running, data export
  freedom.

## Tertiary avatar: "The Privacy Switcher" (10%)

- Any age 18–35; deleting big-tech accounts; found via "local-first apps" / privacy communities;
  buys on the data-sovereignty pitch alone.

## Who this is NOT for (used in sales copy)

- People who want AI coaching, adaptive macro adjustments, or a 19-million-item barcode database.
- People needing medical nutrition therapy or training prescriptions — explicitly out of scope.
- Phone-only users who will never export a file (live syncs are roadmap, not v1).
