# Value Proposition — FitLedger

## Core proposition

**Buy it once. Own it forever. Macros, lifts, and runs in one private app — your data never
leaves your device.**

## The math the buyer does

| | MyFitnessPal Premium | MacroFactor | FitLedger |

## Pain → feature → benefit map

| Buyer pain | Feature | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| "Subscriptions forever for basic tracking" | One-time purchase, full product | Stop renting your own gym log |
| "My data is hostage in 3 apps" | GPX/TCX/CSV import + full CSV/JSON export | Bring 5 years of history in; take everything out, anytime |
| "Apps sell/leak my health data" | Local-first, no account, works offline | Nothing to leak — there is no server |
| "Macro calculators feel like black boxes" | Transparent Mifflin-St Jeor math, editable targets | See the formula, adjust the split, stay in control |
| "Lifting app ≠ running app ≠ food app" | One dashboard, three domains | One place, one streak, one weekly review |
| "Free apps are ad-riddled bait" | No ads, no upsells inside the app | It's a product, not a funnel |

## Honest limitations (stated, not hidden)

- Curated foods database + custom foods — not a 19M-item crowdsourced database.
- No AI/adaptive coaching — it's a precision tool, not a coach.
- Live platform syncs (Strava etc.) are on the public roadmap; v1 connects via standard export
  files. Sales copy must say exactly this.
- Your device holds the data: backups are one click, and the app nags you to take them.
