# Trend Scan — June 2026

**Date:** 2026-06-10 · Citations in `source_notes.md`.

## Macro trends supporting the top opportunities

1. **AI governance urgency is regulation-driven and dated.** EU AI Act penalties begin
   **August 2, 2026** (~8 weeks from now); SMB compliance burden is a documented complaint;
   simplified-template demand is explicitly called for in policy commentary. This is the
   strongest, most time-anchored demand signal found in this research.
2. **SMB AI adoption crossed mainstream threshold.** 68% of US small businesses report regular
   AI use (up from 48% mid-2024); a federal SMB AI training curriculum is rolling out in 2026.
   Adoption without governance creates exactly the pain a policy/rollout kit resolves.
3. **Templates beat courses.** Creator-economy sources consistently report template-style,
   implement-in-30-minutes products outconverting educational content (~3:1 directional claim);
   course fatigue is widely documented. Favors kits/toolkits over a mini-course build.
4. **Business templates carry the best margins** (90–95% directional) and B2B/workflow niches
   are repeatedly named the growth layer vs. saturated consumer printables.
5. **The "free fragment vs. SaaS subscription" gap is recurring.** In AI policy, SOPs, and
   onboarding alike: free single templates (lead magnets) at $0, software at $99–300/mo, and a
   thin middle for complete one-time-purchase kits at $29–149. That middle is where a solo
   seller can win.
6. **Niche-specific beats generic everywhere.** Notion-template market analysis, onboarding-kit
   sellers, and digital-product trend lists all converge: problem-specific products for a
   defined buyer outperform broad "business bundle" products.

## Trend risks

- **AI-governance content velocity:** big HR platforms (SHRM, AIHR, HiBob, Lattice) keep
  publishing free templates; the moat must be completeness + operational depth + updates, not
  the policy document itself.
- **Regulatory change:** EU AI Act guidance, US state AI laws (e.g., Colorado AI Act timeline,
  state patchwork) keep moving — product needs a visible "last reviewed" date and update cadence
  (this maps to Phase 7's market-adaptation system and is an advantage for an updatable digital
  product).
- **Collector-car niche cyclicality:** collector market activity varies with the economy; the
  documentation pain persists (records always increase sale credibility) but listing volume can
  shrink in downturns.
- **Generic-template saturation:** any idea executed generically (consumer printables, broad
  social templates) enters the saturated layer — avoided by the brief's B2B/high-pain rule.

## v2 addendum — fitness/athletic niche (owner-directed revision)

1. **Fitness entrepreneurship is the demand layer, not fitness consumption.** The 18–35 cohort
   drives 60% of discretionary fitness-spending growth, and a visible founder economy has formed
   around it: activewear brand launches (a ~$340–460B market), online coaching (>$100B online
   fitness by 2030), fitness UGC, run clubs. Digital products that serve these *builders* fit
   the brief's B2B/high-pain rule; products that serve *exercisers* (workout PDFs) are
   medical-adjacent and saturated.
2. **Activewear launch pain is structural, not trendy.** Manufacturers require tech packs;
   freelancers charge $150–2,000+; first runs cost $5,000–15,000 and take 16–24 weeks. None of
   this is cyclical — it's how garment production works, which makes a launch kit evergreen.
3. **Cheap AI tech-pack gigs ($5 Fiverr) are a commoditization signal** for the document alone —
   reinforcing that the defensible product is the *system + judgment* (vetting, QC, costing,
   sequencing), not the template file.
4. **Operator-side Gen Z anxiety is peaking** (gymtimidation, contract aversion, failed-payment
   churn, AI trust gap) — strong but more time-bound than F1's pain.

## Timing verdict

A regulation-anchored AI governance kit is unusually well-timed (deadline ~Aug 2, 2026 creates a
real "why now"); the collector-car toolkit is timing-neutral/evergreen; the onboarding kit is
timing-neutral with proven steady demand.
