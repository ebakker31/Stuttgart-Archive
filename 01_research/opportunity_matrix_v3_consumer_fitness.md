# Opportunity Matrix v3 — Consumer Fitness Products (18–35)

**Date:** 2026-06-10 · **Owner direction:** target consumers (fitness enthusiasts themselves),
not B2B buyers. Kept legally safe: organization/tracking/planning/calculation tools only — **no
training programs, no nutrition prescriptions, no medical-adjacent advice.**

**Honest trade-off note (per original brief):** consumer products price lower ($9–39 vs $49–149
B2B) and refund/competition pressure is higher. Compensation strategy: pick consumer niches where
the buyer has already spent heavily (race entries, home-gym budgets) so a small tool price is
trivial relative to their committed spend.

## Scoring rationale highlights

- **C1 Hybrid Race-Prep Tracking System (74):** The strongest consumer trend found. HYROX-style
  fitness racing has tens of thousands of yearly participants and ~1M logged race results;
  a whole data ecosystem (HyCoach, HyroxDataLab, race calculators) sprang up in 2026, proving
  athletes obsess over splits, pacing, and benchmarks. Buyers are *race-entered* — they've paid
  significant entry fees and train for months, so $19–29 for a complete tracking/pacing/race-day
  system is trivial. Existing products are apps, free pacing articles, or coaching programs;
  no polished owned-file tracking system found. Legal care: descriptive use of the HYROX
  trademark only ("compatible with HYROX-style races", no logos/affiliation implication), and
  strictly tracking/calculation — the athlete brings their own training plan. Trend risk: tied
  to the hybrid-racing wave (strong through 2026, not guaranteed forever).
- **C2 Home Gym Build Planner (73):** Builds cost $800–$25k+ and planning mistakes are
  expensive; free content is fragmented blog guides — no interactive planner/calculator product
  found. Evergreen, zero advice risk (purchasing decisions, not exercise). Slightly weaker "why
  now" than C1; ~1/3 of US adults plan equipment purchases.
- **C3 Progressive Overload Tracker (68):** Proven Etsy/TikTok demand and the anti-subscription
  angle resonates with 18–35, but it's the most crowded of the three and hardest to
  differentiate beyond formula quality and design.
- **C5–C8 scored down:** saturation (planners, challenge kits), trademark risk (75 Hard),
  nutrition-advice adjacency (meal prep), or weak willingness to pay.
