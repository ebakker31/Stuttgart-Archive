# Competitor Scan

**Date:** 2026-06-10 · Citations in `source_notes.md`. Focused on the top-scoring ideas.

## Idea #1 — SMB AI Governance & Adoption Toolkit

| Competitor type | Examples | Price | Gap they leave |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free single policy templates | SHRM, AIHR, HiBob, Lattice, Optro, Fisher Phillips sample PDF | $0 (often email-gated lead magnets) | One document, no system: no applicability checklist, risk register, vendor assessment, rollout plan, or training materials; HR-platform templates assume their software |
| Law-firm engagements | Fisher Phillips et al. | $thousands | Unaffordable for most SMBs; slow |
| Compliance SaaS | AIComply, Prediction Guard-reviewed enterprise tools | ~$99–300+/mo or enterprise pricing | Subscription overkill for an SMB that needs documents + a process, not a platform |
| EU official resources | artificialintelligenceact.eu compliance checker, SME guide | $0 | EU-only scope, no US/state coverage, informational not operational — nothing fill-in-ready |

**Positioning gap:** a complete, one-time-purchase, plain-English **kit** (policy pack + applicability
checklist + risk register + vendor assessment + rollout/training assets) priced between "free
fragment" and "SaaS subscription." Must be explicit it is an operational starting point to review
with counsel, not legal advice.

## Idea #2 — Collector Car Sale-Prep & Documentation Toolkit

| Competitor type | Examples | Price | Gap they leave |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single-document PDFs | Etsy classic-car sale agreement templates | ~$5–20 | One form, no documentation system; legal-form quality varies |
| Free blog checklists | Gateway Classic Cars, Ideal Classic Cars, Auto Retro Passion | $0 | Not actionable files; buyer-side only; no records organization |
| Ebook + checklist | GuysWithRides "How To Sell Your Classic Car Online" (90-point inspection list) | low-ticket | Selling-process advice, not a records/provenance system |
| VIN/build-sheet services | Classic Decoder | per-lookup | Data lookup, not organization or presentation |
| Full platforms | **Stuttgart Archive (this repo)** — adjacent, not a competitor | free/paid tiers | A downloadable toolkit is complementary: works offline, no signup, and can funnel buyers to the platform |

**Positioning gap:** no complete "document, organize, and present your collector car for sale"
system exists as a digital product. Differentiation is strongest of all 13 ideas; market size is
the open question (niche).

## v2 (fitness niche) — Idea F1: Activewear Brand Launch Kit

| Competitor type | Examples | Price | Gap they leave |
|---|---|---|---|
| Template stores | TechPackTemplates.com, FittDesign | ~$20–60 per garment template | Single tech pack files only — no sourcing, sampling, costing, or launch process |
| Etsy listings | Activewear tech pack market; standout 65-POM Excel template by 15-yr head of design | ~$10–50 | Same: document, not system; quality varies widely |
| Fiverr services | $5 AI-generated to $110 custom tech packs | $5–110 | Done-for-you single garments; cheap tier is low-trust AI output; no founder education or process |
| Freelance designers | Standard market rate | $150–500/garment, $500–2,000+ complex | Expensive per-garment; the kit's price anchor |
| Free templates | BOMME Studio, Human B, template.net | $0 | Generic (not activewear-specific grading/seams), lead magnets, single files |
| Sourcing guides | China Fitness Clothing hub et al. | $0 (content marketing) | Manufacturer-side content with sourcing bias; not neutral or actionable files |

**Positioning gap:** every competitor sells or gives away a *piece* (one tech pack, one guide).
No complete neutral "idea → tech packs → manufacturer → sample → cost → launch" system found as
a one-time digital product. Price anchor is strong: the kit replaces a $500–2,000 freelancer
line-item and de-risks a $5,000–15,000 first run.

## Other scanned ideas (summary)

- **SOP packs (#5):** Operations Mavenue sells a 60+ SOP manual (one-time, Notion) — direct,
  established competitor; Scribe/Smartsheet/Asana give strong free alternatives.
- **Employee onboarding (#6):** FirstHR, Asana, monday.com, Venngage, HR Cloud all give free
  templates; differentiation would have to be "no-HR-department" depth.
- **Sales enablement (#7):** Demand Metric (47 tools), Highspot, Salesforce flood the space with
  free/gated content; vendor content is high quality, hard to out-credential.
- **Pricing calculators (#11):** multiple polished free options — commoditized.
- **Mini-courses (#12):** course fatigue documented; templates outsell ~3:1.
