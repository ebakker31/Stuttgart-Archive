**Date:** 2026-06-10 · **Owner direction:** "New ideas related to fitness / athletic niches,
clothing etc., age demographic 18–35. Something really needed in the industry."

advice (rules out consumer workout/nutrition programs), B2B/high-pain preferred. The strongest
fits are digital products sold to **people building businesses in fitness** — apparel founders,
gym/studio operators, coaches, and fitness creators, who are overwhelmingly 18–35.

## Scoring rationale highlights

- **F1 Activewear Brand Launch Kit (78):** The validated pain is expensive and specific —
  manufacturers require tech packs, freelancers charge **$150–500 per garment ($500–2,000+
  complex)**, first production runs cost **$5,000–15,000**, and the zero-to-product timeline is
  **16–24 weeks** with many failure points (bad tech packs, bad sampling, bad manufacturer
  picks). Competition exists for *single tech pack templates* (Etsy, FittDesign,
  TechPackTemplates.com, $5–110 Fiverr gigs) but no complete *launch system* was found — the
  same "fragments vs. system" gap as v1's winner. Buyer is squarely 18–35 (launching an
  activewear brand is a flagship Gen Z/millennial ambition; activewear is a ~$340–460B market
  still growing). Digital, legal-safe, evergreen, updatable, strong upsell path (ops kit F3 is
  a natural premium tier). Suggested price $59–129.
- **F2 Gym & Studio Gen Z Playbook (75):** Strong documented operator pain — Gen Z/millennials
  drive 60% of discretionary fitness-spending growth, yet operators report gymtimidation,
  contract aversion, and retention problems (failed payments drive up to 1 in 3 cancellations).
  B2B buyer with real budget. Slightly weaker evergreen (generational framing dates) and the
  big chains' free content is decent. Suggested price $49–99.
- **F4 vs F6 vs F7:** all viable but lower ceilings — creators (F4) and club organizers (F7)
  have low willingness to pay; coaches (F6) are increasingly served by all-in-one software
  (Trainerize, Virtuagym et al.) that bundles onboarding/check-ins, and waiver/health-screen
  content brushes against liability.
- **F8 rejected** on the brief's own rules (no medical-adjacent consumer advice; avoid the
  saturated generic layer).

## Revised top 3 recommendation

1. **Option 1 — Activewear Brand Launch Kit** (78): clothing + fitness + 18–35 exactly as
   directed; expensive validated pain; clear differentiation gap.
2. **Option 2 — Gym & Studio Gen Z Playbook** (75): strongest trend support; B2B operator
   budget; fast build.
3. **Option 3 — Fitness Creator / UGC Business Kit** (68): purest 18–35 demographic fit;
   fastest build; lowest price ceiling.
